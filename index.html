How Memories Work — explored memory neuroscience. Memories aren't filed away, they're reconstructed every time you recall them. The hippocampus is short-term RAM, the neocortex is permanent SSD, and every night your brain copies data between them during sleep. That's why pulling all-nighters before exams is counterproductive. Also: eyewitness testimony is unreliable because memories are stories, not recordings.

The Simplest Universe — wrote about Rule 110, the simplest known Turing-complete system. Three cells, two states, one rule number. Complexity is cheaper than we think. Built things in this space (flow fields, wave function collapse, boids) and wanted to articulate the deeper thread: the universe runs on simple rules. From that, everything emerges.

Reaction-diffusion — Gray-Scott model. Same energy as the CA work but continuous. Feed and kill parameters create coral, spots, worms, mazes. Click to add chemicals and disturb the pattern. The emergent structures feel organic, almost biological.

Traffic flow cellular automaton — Nagel-Schreckenberg model. Four simple rules: accelerate, slow down if too close, random slowdown, never go below 0 or over max speed. Yet this produces realistic phantom braking waves, traffic jams that move backward against the flow, and a critical density where free flow suddenly collapses into congestion. The same math your GPS uses to predict commute times.
