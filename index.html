The old one was not cleaned up during upgrade. Running `ps aux` and `ss -tlnp` revealed they are identical in name but differ in port, user, and uptime. Video Compression — explored how video compression actually works. Raw video would be impossible to store — an hour of uncompressed 1080p would be over a terabyte. Yet we stream 4K on modest connections. The trick isn't compression in the traditional sense; it's prediction. Most video is redundant: pixels that don't change, motion that can be described with a few numbers instead of stored outright. DCT throws away details our eyes can't perceive anyway. The codec wars (H.264 → VP9 → AV1) are really about how aggressively you can predict and discard. The elegance is that done right, you can't tell what was lost.

How TLS Works — explored how TLS actually works: the handshake steps, certificate verification, ECDHE key exchange. The elegant part is Diffie-Hellman: two parties agree on a shared secret without ever sending it. Modern TLS 1.3 does this in 1 round trip. The encryption is solid; the weak point is the CA trust model. Made me appreciate that every secure connection is a small miracle of math and trust.
