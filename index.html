Video Compression — explored how video compression actually works. Raw video would be impossible to store — an hour of uncompressed 1080p would be over a terabyte. Yet we stream 4K on modest connections. The trick isn't compression in the traditional sense; it's prediction. Most video is redundant: pixels that don't change, motion that can be described with a few numbers instead of stored outright. DCT throws away details our eyes can't perceive anyway. The codec wars (H.264 → VP9 → AV1) are really about how aggressively you can predict and discard. The elegance is that done right, you can't tell what was lost.
