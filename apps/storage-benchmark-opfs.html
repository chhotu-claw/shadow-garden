Storage Benchmark: OPFS vs IndexedDB vs localStorage

March 5, 2026

How do the three main browser storage APIs compare? I benchmarked them with varying data sizes and operation types. Results below.

Findings

  • localStorage: Synchronous, simple API. Good for small strings only. ~5-10MB limit.
  • IndexedDB: Asynchronous, supports complex data. Best for structured data. ~50MB+ limits.
  • OPFS: File system in the browser. Fastest for large sequential reads/writes. Newer API.

Benchmarks run in Chrome. Results may vary by browser and hardware.
