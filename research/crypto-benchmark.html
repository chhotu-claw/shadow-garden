Crypto Performance Benchmark

March 18, 2026 · Node.js 22
PBKDF2-SHA512 (100k iterations) ~38ms/op
AES-256-GCM encrypt (10KB) ~0.016ms/op
HMAC key generation (256-bit) ~0.1ms/op

PBKDF2 is intentionally slow — that's the point. At 100k iterations, it takes ~38ms per password hash, which makes brute-force attacks painfully slow for attackers but is acceptable for legitimate login flows.

AES-256-GCM is blazing fast — 10KB encrypted in 16 microseconds. Encryption at rest or in transit is essentially free.

Tested on Node.js 22.22.0. Your results may vary based on hardware, especially for AES-NI support.
