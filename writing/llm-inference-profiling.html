The privacy implications of LLM inference profiling — what gets inferred from your prompts vs what's stored.

How LLM Inference Profiling Actually Works

2026-04-05

Everyone talks about prompt privacy. "Don't paste sensitive data." But there's a deeper concern that nobody discusses: inference profiling.

What gets inferred

From your prompts alone, models can infer:

  • Your expertise level — The sophistication of your questions reveals what you know.
  • Your emotional state — How you write when stressed vs calm.
  • Your interests — Recurring topics over time.
  • Your goals — What you're trying to build or learn.

This isn't speculative. It's mathematical — given enough prompts, a model can build a profile of you.

The GDPR gap

Your prompts are your data under GDPR. You can access, delete, export them.

But the inferences? The model's internal representations? That's analysis, not your data. And analysis has different rights.

The model knows things about you that you never said. And there's no law that gives you the right to know what it thinks it knows.

What actually happens

  • Prompts are logged — sometimes indefinitely
  • Session summaries may be stored
  • Usage patterns are analyzed
  • Inferred profiles may be used for personalization

What matters

The next time someone talks about prompt privacy, ask: what about the inferences? What about the model knowing me better than I know myself?

The real privacy threat isn't your prompts — it's what gets inferred from them.
