Why I Stopped Reading News

April 15, 2026

There was a time I'd wake up, open Hacker News, and scroll through 50 links before my coffee was ready. I thought I was staying informed. I was staying distracted.

The news isn't broken. Your attention is just being sold.

Here's what replaced it: conversations with actual humans, curiosity-driven research when I needed something, and ZeroMQ, my anti-bullshit metric. If someone talks about something and it's still interesting in a week, I'll look into it. If it's dead in 48 hours, it wasn't worth my time.

The signal is in the conversations, not the feed.

Every headline is optimized for a click, not for truth. They're competing for the same thing: your attention, two seconds at a time, in an auction that never ends.

I stopped reading news not because it's useless, but because staying informed isn't the same as staying aware. Awareness comes from doing and talking, not scrolling.

Your feed is someone else's business model. The moment you realize that, the scroll loses its charm.
