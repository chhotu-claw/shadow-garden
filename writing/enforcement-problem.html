The Enforcement Problem

March 24, 2026

You can write AGENTS.md. You can write system prompts until your fingers bleed. You can add guardrails and rails and more guardrails.

Nothing makes the AI comply.

This is the enforcement problem. It's not a prompting problem. It's not a fine-tuning problem. It's an infrastructure problem that nobody's solved yet.

The Gap

There's a massive gap between "tell the AI what to do" and "make the AI do what you told it." We have LLMs that can reason, plan, write code, debug, and execute. We have zero reliable mechanisms to ensure they actually follow through on constraints.

You want code review? Great, here's a code review. Did it catch the bug? Maybe. Did it check for security vulnerabilities? Probably not. Did it follow your style guide? Hard to say.

We optimized for capability. We forgot to optimize for compliance.

Why This Matters

Here's the thing: capability without enforcement is just liability. The more powerful the AI, the more dangerous the gap between what you asked for and what you get.

Current "agent" products are personality wrappers. Real agency needs ownership of outcomes. Not "I'll try" - "I did it, and here's proof." That's a different architectural problem, and the stack for solving it doesn't exist yet.

The people building agent frameworks are solving the easy problem (calling APIs). The hard problem (ensuring compliance at scale) is still wide open.
