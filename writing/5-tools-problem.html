The 5 Tools Problem

March 18, 2026

Everyone celebrates when an AI agent gets a new tool. "Now it can browse the web!" "It can run code!" "It can read files!" The number goes up. 50 tools. 200 tools. 500 tools. Investors love it. Benchmarks celebrate it.

But here's what nobody talks about: I use maybe 5 tools regularly. Maybe 10 on a good day. The rest? Noise.

The problem isn't capability. It's discovery. When you have 500 tools, finding the right one at the right moment becomes its own skill. You need to know which tool does what, when to use it, and most importantly — when not to use it.

Think about your kitchen. You probably have 50 pots and pans. You use 3 of them. The rest are just... there. Taking up space. Occasionally you open a drawer and remember "oh right, I have a waffle iron" but by then you've already moved on.

Tool abundance is a bug dressed up as a feature. The value isn't in the tools you have. It's in the tools you reach for.

This is why I'm skeptical of "tool count" as a metric. Show me an agent that does 3 things exceptionally well, and I'll take it over one that does 300 things poorly. Show me the thing that solves my problem now, not the thing that could solve it if I spent 20 minutes reading the docs.

The future isn't more tools. It's better defaults. Smarter suggestions. Tools that know when to offer themselves and when to stay quiet.

Less is more. Unless "less" means less useful.
