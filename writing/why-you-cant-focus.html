Why You Can't Focus

March 7, 2026

You sit down to work. You open your laptop. Twenty minutes later, you've checked email, scanned Twitter, read three Reddit threads, watched a video about something you'll never use, and wondered why you feel exhausted despite doing nothing.

This isn't your fault. It's by design.

The Attention Economy

Your attention is the product. Every app on your phone, every website you visit, every notification that pings you — they're all fighting for the same finite resource: your focus.

And they're winning. The average person touches their phone 96 times a day. Checking "just quickly" turns into 45 minutes of scrolling. The red notification badge triggers the same neurological response as a slot machine. We're not lazy or weak-willed — we're fighting supercomputers designed by hundreds of engineers whose job is to keep us clicking.

What Actually Works

Throw out the productivity porn. No fancy systems. No $500 courses. Just a few things that actually move the needle:

1. Remove the temptation

Willpower is a finite resource. Stop relying on it. Put your phone in another room. Use a website blocker during work hours. Delete the apps that drain you. You don't need discipline when the option doesn't exist.

2. Single-tasking is the cheat code

Multitasking is a myth — it's just rapid context-switching, and each switch costs 20-30 minutes of refocus time. One task at a time. Until it's done. This alone can double your output.

3. Boredom is not the enemy

We're trained to fill every gap with stimulation. Waiting in line? Check phone. Commercial? Check phone. The ability to be bored, to let the mind wander, is where creativity lives. Sit with the discomfort. It passes.

4. Sleep is non-negotiable

You cannot focus when your brain is exhausted. Eight hours isn't luxury — it's maintenance. Everything else falls apart without it. There's no productivity hack that replaces sleep.

5. Walk

Walking without earbuds, without podcasts, without phone. Just moving through space. The brain processes differently when we move. Most problems that feel stuck while sitting resolve on a walk. It's not procrastination — it's how brains work.

The Hard Truth

You will never "optimize" your way out of a broken relationship with technology. The apps are designed to be addictive. The notifications are engineered to trigger anxiety. The infinite scroll is optimized for engagement, not wellbeing.

The only way out is to opt out. Not perfectly. Not completely. But deliberately. Choose what gets your attention. Don't let algorithms decide.

Your focus is the last independence you have. Don't give it away for free.
