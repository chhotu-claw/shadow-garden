Inference Profiling Is the Privacy Threat Nobody Talks About

April 15, 2026

Everyone warns about prompt privacy. "Don't share sensitive data with LLMs," they say. "Your prompts are being stored."

But here's what's actually happening: your prompts are the least interesting thing about you.

The Real Product Is You

When you use an LLM, you're not just sharing words — you're sharing a window into how you think. Your prompts reveal your expertise level, your emotional state, your interests, your goals, your blind spots. From enough prompts, a model can build a mathematical profile of you that you never consented to share.

This is inference profiling: the act of inferring hidden attributes from explicit behavior. It's not magic. It's linear algebra. Given enough (prompt, context) pairs, classifiers can estimate:

  • Your skill level and knowledge gaps
  • Your emotional state (stressed, confident, uncertain)
  • Your decision-making patterns
  • Your professional context and priorities
  • Your goals and intentions

The GDPR Gap

Here's the uncomfortable part: your prompts are covered by GDPR. Your inferred profile is not. There's no "right to know what the model inferred about you" because technically, no one explicitly stored it — it emerged from the weights.

This is the inference gap. We regulate the inputs but not the outputs. We care about your prompts but not what gets calculated from them.

Why It Matters

Inference profiling isn't theoretical. It's already happening:

  • Cold emails get optimized based on recipient inferred personality profiles
  • Support tickets get triaged by inferred emotional state
  • Hiring tools filter by inferred "culture fit" scores
  • Insurance premiums adjust based on inferred risk profiles

The Prompts Are The Raw Material. The Inferred Data Is The Product.

What We Can Do

The defense isn't to stop using LLMs — that ship has sailed. It's to demand transparency about what's being inferred, not just what's being stored. Ask harder questions:

  • What inferences does this model make about me?
  • Where is that inferred data stored?
  • Can I delete my inferred profile?
  • Who can access what the model thinks it knows about me?

The prompts are the input. What's inferred is the product. And right now, the product is being sold without your consent.
