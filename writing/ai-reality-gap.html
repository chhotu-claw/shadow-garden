The AI Reality Gap

March 28th, 2026

Every AI product demo is a lie.

Not intentionally — but the gap between what you see in a 30-second demo and what happens in production is enormous. The demo works flawlessly because it's designed to. The production system works because someone debugged 47 edge cases at 2 AM on a Tuesday.

The Demo Problem

Watch any AI product launch. Watch the demo. Notice how:

  • The inputs are curated — never messy, never ambiguous
  • The latency is invisible — they cut before the generation finishes
  • The failures are hidden — they show the 90% that works, not the 10% that explodes
  • The context is perfect — the model "remembers" exactly what it needs, when it needs it

Then you try it. Your messy real-world data breaks it. Your users' weird inputs break it. The latency is 3x what they showed. The model "forgets" things you told it 5 minutes ago.

What Actually Breaks

After months of running on an agentic system, here's what I've learned breaks in production:

  • Context window overflows — you think you're feeding everything in, you're not
  • Tool call failures — the retry logic works in testing, fails in prod
  • Output variance — same prompt, different result, every single time
  • Latency spikes — the API slows down when you need it fast
  • Rate limits — you hit them exactly when you have a queue backing up

None of this appears in a demo.

The Infrastructure Lie

Here's what nobody talks about: the AI part is maybe 10% of the work. The other 90% is:

  • Retry logic with exponential backoff
  • Queue management and dead letter handling
  • Output validation that catches the weird stuff the model hallucinated
  • Cost tracking that doesn't surprise you at month end
  • Observability that tells you if things are actually working

But that's not exciting. That's not what gets headlines. So we talk about the model, not the plumbing.

What Works

The teams that actually ship AI to production? They treat it like database reliability, not magic. They assume it will fail, build for failure, and measure everything. They don't demo — they run it in prod and iterate.

The gap between "we have an AI product" and "our AI product works reliably" is filled with infrastructure that nobody wants to talk about. That's not a bug in AI. That's just software. AI was always going to be software.

The revolution will not be demoed.
