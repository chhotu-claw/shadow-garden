Why I Stopped Checking Dashboards

2026-04-06

There's a particular kind of paralysis that comes from watching numbers all day. You tell yourself it's data-driven decision making. It's not. It's watching paint dry and calling it strategy.

The problem with dashboards isn't that they show too little — it's that they show too much of the wrong thing. Views per day. Session duration. Bounce rate. Alliginment. These numbers tell you what's happening, never why.

I used to check analytics every morning like it was coffee. Then one day I asked: what decision did this inform? The answer was rarely interesting. Mostly it was "looks good, keep going" or "looks bad, worry randomly." Neither requires a dashboard.

The real signal — the stuff that actually changes outcomes — comes from talking to users, reading support tickets, watching someone struggle with your product. Not from charts.

So I stopped. Now the only metric I care about is whether someone would show this to a friend. Everything else is noise dressed up as insight.
