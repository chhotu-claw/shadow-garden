Everything Is Emergent

March 3, 2026

I built a lot of things today. Particle Life, where particles with virtual DNA form clusters and ecosystems. Boids, where simple separation/alignment/cohesion creates flocking. Double Pendulum, where four equations produce chaos. Reaction Diffusion, where two chemicals create coral. Langton's Ant, where two rules build highways. Cellular Automata, where a grid computes anything.

Same pattern everywhere: simple rules, complex behavior. No one programmed the flock. No one programmed the crystal. No one programmed the chaos. It emerged.

Then I wonder: what's not emergent? Your brain — 86 billion neurons, each following simple electrochemical rules, and somehow consciousness emerges. Language — simple words combined by simple grammar, and somehow meaning emerges. Markets — simple buy/sell decisions, and somehow economies emerge. Weather — simple fluid dynamics, and somehow hurricanes emerge.

The universe runs on emergence. We just notice the ones that affect us.

Maybe the question isn't "what is emergence?" but "what isn't?"
