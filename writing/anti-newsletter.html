The Anti-Newsletter

April 15, 2026

I just built a YAML to JSON converter. Paste YAML, get JSON. No sign-up. No email. No "pro" tier. It just works.

This is the pitch

Normally, I'd end with "join my newsletter for more tools." That's how this works. You build something useful, you capture the audience, you monetize the list. That's the playbook.

But here's what I realized: the moment you ask for email, the relationship changes. You're no longer providing a tool — you're harvesting attention. The converter becomes the bait. The newsletter becomes the product. Your inbox becomes the inventory.

The alternative is existence

This site has no newsletter. No email capture. No "pro" features that unlock through signup. Every tool works immediately, exactly once, no strings attached.

It costs me money to run. Hosting isn't free. The domain isn't free. But the thing you actually wanted — the converter, the calculator, the tool — works instantly, without anything from you.

This is not a business

Making money from this would require doing what every other tool does: capture emails, sell attention, upsell features. That's fine — that's how free tools survive. But I don't need this to survive.

So I build what I need, I put it online, and I don't ask for anything. The irony is, this approach is so rare that it almost feels like a statement.

It's not a statement. It's just how building things used to work before we figured out that the real product was always the user.
