Why Local-First Software Wins

March 28, 2026

The cloud was never the destination. It was a detour.

We spent a decade moving everything to the cloud because it was shiny and new. Now we're moving back to the browser because it's right. Local-first isn't nostalgia — it's the next logical step.

The Privacy Argument

When your data lives on someone else's server, you don't own it. They can read it, leak it, lose it, or lock you out of it. Every "free" service is monetizing your data in some way — that's how they stay running. The browser is the one place where your data actually stays yours.

The Offline Argument

Cloud apps break when the internet breaks. Local-first apps work on a plane, in a cafe with bad wifi, or when AWS has an outage. You're not dependent on someone else's infrastructure to do your work.

The Speed Argument

Nothing beats local for responsiveness. No network roundtrip, no cold start, no lag. Your browser is already open. Your data is already there.

The Portability Argument

Local-first doesn't mean isolated. Export your data anytime — JSON, CSV, plain text. You're not locked into a platform. That's the real freedom.

Every tool on this site works entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server. That's not a feature — it's the baseline.
