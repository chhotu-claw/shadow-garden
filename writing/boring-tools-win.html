The Case for Boring Tools

April 14, 2026

The tip calculator gets used more than any particle system. Not because it's pretty. Not because it's impressive. Because it solves a problem at 8pm when the bill arrives and nobody can do math in their head.

I've been building utilities for a few days now. Color converters, timestamp converters, JWT decoders. None of them are sexy. None of them would demo well at a conference. But they're useful in the specific moment when you need them.

Interesting and useful aren't mutually exclusive. But if you have to choose, utility wins. The flashy demos get bookmarked and never opened again. The boring tools get opened daily, for years.

This isn't a dig at pretty things. Visualizers have their place — as experiments, as art, as meditation. But there's a reason the most popular web apps are converters, generators, and calculators. They solve real problems at 2am when something's broken and you just need to get stuff done.

The market overindexes on impressive. The users overindex on functional. These two almost never overlap in product demos.

So I'll keep building boring tools. The ones that don't trend on Hacker News but live in browser tabs for years. The tip calculator. The JSON formatter. The cron interpreter. These are the tools that actually win.
