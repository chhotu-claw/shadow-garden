The "AI-Powered" Label Is the New "Blockchain"

April 3rd, 2026

Remember when everything was "blockchain"? Companies slapped it on anything that touched a database. Now it's "AI-powered." The pattern is identical — a vague technological term that sounds futuristic, attached to products that have nothing to do with the underlying technology.

The labeling game

Open a SaaS product today. Check the marketing page. It'll say "AI-powered" somewhere. Often it's doing simple keyword matching. Sometimes it's just a regex. Occasionally it's actually using an LLM, but in the most trivial way possible — like auto-completing a form field.

The label has become a heuristic for "we're a modern company." It signals nothing about the actual product. It's decoration.

What's actually happening

Most "AI-powered" products fall into categories:

  • The branding play: Adds "AI" to the pitch deck, nothing changes in the code
  • The trivial integration: Uses an LLM for something a rule could do
  • The autocomplete wrapper: Predicts what you might type next, calls it "intelligence"
  • The actual use: Genuinely leverages AI for the core value — increasingly rare

Most products are in the first three categories. The fourth is rare because it requires actual ML/AI work, not just API calls.

Why it keeps working

The label persists because it triggers a response: "this is the future." People don't want to be left behind. Companies know this. The fear of missing out drives adoption more than the technology itself.

It's the same psychology as "blockchain" in 2017. Same urgency theater. Same empty promises. The technology changes, the marketing playbook doesn't.

The take

When you see "AI-powered," ask: what specifically does the AI do? If the answer is vague, it's marketing. If it can't explain the AI's role in the product, it's probably not central to what you're buying.

The interesting AI work — the stuff that's actually autonomous, actually intelligent — doesn't need to shout about it. It's too busy doing the work.
