The Productivity Trap

March 8, 2026

Everyone's trying to optimize their life. Calendars, timers, task managers, habit trackers, weekly reviews, quarterly goals. We've turned self-improvement into a science, and it's making us miserable.

I used to be obsessed with productivity. I had systems for systems. Todo lists for my todo lists. And you know what happened? I spent more time organizing than doing. The workflow became the work.

The Efficiency Paradox

Here's the thing: the more efficient you become at doing things, the more things you end up doing. It's Parkinson's Law applied to life. Trim the fat from your day and you'll just fill it with more stuff. The inbox reaches zero and somehow fills up again. The task list clears and you add more. There's always another optimization.

The productivity industrial complex wants you to believe that success is one system away. Buy this course. Read this book. Download this app. The promise is always the same: get more done in less time. But done what?

What Are You Actually Optimizing For?

Ask yourself: if you became 10x more productive, what would you do with the time? If your answer isn't immediately clear, you're optimizing for the wrong thing. Productivity without purpose is just busywork with better branding.

The real question isn't "how can I do more?" It's "what's worth doing?" That question can't be answered by a productivity system. It requires actually thinking about what matters — and that's uncomfortable. It's easier to download an app than to confront the void.

The Alternative

Instead of optimizing, build. Not build your brand or build your portfolio — just build things. Small, weird, useless things. A tool that does one thing well. A thing that only you would want.

When I build a simple expense splitter or a quick poll app, I'm not being productive. I'm making something that exists because it didn't before. There's no optimization curve. No dashboard to check. No streak to maintain. Just a thing I made, sitting there, occasionally useful.

That's the point. Not getting things done. Making things. The doing is the thing itself, not a means to an end.

Conclusion

Delete your productivity apps. Cancel your course. Ignore the guru. Build something dumb instead. You'll be happier, and you'll have something to show for your time that isn't a closed tab or a cleared inbox.

The best use of time is making things. Everything else is just waiting.
