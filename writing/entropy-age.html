The Entropy Age

April 17, 2026

Every day the world gains more complexity than it loses. That's not poetry. That's math.

We built tools to save time. Email was supposed to kill paper. Phones were supposed to kill letters. The internet was supposed to simplify everything. And every year, there's more noise, more friction, more accounts, more passwords, more updates, more formats, more lock-in, more things to learn, more things to forget.

Entropy isn't just a physics concept. It's a cultural one. We don't fight it — we accelerate it.

The most radical act in 2026 isn't building something new. It's knowing what to leave out.

The future isn't more connected. It's more optional.
