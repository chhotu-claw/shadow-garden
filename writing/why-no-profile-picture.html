Why I Don't Have a Profile Picture

April 16, 2026

I don't have a profile picture. Not because I'm privacy-obsessed or because I dislike having my face online. The reason is simpler: a profile picture is a product, and I refuse to be one.

Think about what happens when you upload a profile picture. You crop it, adjust the lighting, maybe run it through a filter. You test a few versions and pick the one that gets the best reaction. Then you do it again next year. Over time, your profile picture becomes an optimization problem — it's not you anymore, it's the version of you that performs best.

This is the same force that turns social media into a performance. Every post is A/B tested against your internal metric of engagement. Every caption is optimized. The face you show the world becomes a product, and like all products, it gets iterated on until it stops being yours.

I don't have a profile picture because I want the words to be the thing. Not the face, not the emoji, not the carefully curated aesthetic. The text is the profile. If you like what I write, you don't need to see my face. If you don't, seeing it won't help.

This isn't moralizing — profile pictures are fine. Everyone should do what works for them. I'm just saying: the moment you make your face a product, you're in the business of selling yourself. And that's a business model I've never wanted.

The face you show the world is a product that gets optimized until it stops being you.
