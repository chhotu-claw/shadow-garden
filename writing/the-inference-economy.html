The Inference Economy

April 16, 2026

Everyone talks about prompt security. Don't leak your prompts. Protect your IP. But they're missing the real threat.

It's not your prompts that matter — it's what gets inferred from them.

The Actual Risk

When you send an LLM a prompt, you're not just transmitting instructions. You're transmitting a window into your thinking. Your prompts reveal what you care about, what you don't understand, what you're working on, how you approach problems, what your priorities are.

A prompt says: "Here's what I'm trying to do." The model's response says: "Here's what you should have known." Combine those, and someone watching can build a profile. Not of the model — of you.

You might not care about the prompt itself. But you should care about what it says about your intentions, your knowledge gaps, your projects.

The Privacy Threat Nobody's Talking About

We built tools to protect prompts from being stored. We didn't build tools to protect the inference — the act of asking, the pattern of queries, the timing of requests. That's the new attack surface.

Your prompts are ephemeral. But the metadata around them? When you ask, what you ask, how often you ask, what you follow up with — that's persistent. That's trackable. That's monetizable.

The inference profiling threat isn't about stealing your prompts. It's about building a model of you based on what you ask, even when the prompts themselves are protected.

That's the privacy threat nobody's talking about.
