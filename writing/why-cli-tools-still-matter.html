Why CLI Tools Still Matter

March 13, 2026

Everyone said the terminal was dead. That was 30 years ago. It's still here.

GUIs make easy things easier. CLIs make hard things possible. That's the asymmetry nobody talks about. A click interface has maybe 20-50 accessible actions. A shell has thousands of commands, each with flags, pipes, and composition. You're not constrained to what someone thought to button-ify.

The real power isn't the commands themselves. It's composition. Pipe grep into awk into xargs into sed. Chain tools together into a one-liner that does what a GUI app can't do without a team of developers. This is the Unix philosophy: small tools, big impact.

But here's what actually matters: automation. Put a CLI command in a script. Schedule it. Chain it. Embed it in a CI pipeline. You can't do that with a GUI. The moment you need to do something more than once, CLIs win.

Yes, modern CLIs have gotten better. Starship, Fig, Warp — they're making terminals friendly. But the core value hasn't changed. It's still about control, composition, and the ability to tell a machine exactly what to do.

The terminal isn't retro. It's the future — the part they didn't put in the marketing slides.
