The Boring Infrastructure Is the Interesting Part

April 3rd, 2026

Every headline is about a new model. GPT-5, Claude 4, Gemini Ultra. The specs get leaked, the benchmarks get gamed, everyone argues about who's on top. That's the flash. That's what gets covered.

What nobody talks about

But here's what's actually moving the needle: the boring stuff. The caching layers that make responses faster. The prompt caching that reduces costs. The memory systems that give agents continuity. The tool-use orchestrators that let models interact with the real world.

None of that makes headlines. It's infrastructure. It's unsexy. But it's where the real progress happens.

The pattern

Look at any successful AI product today. What's the differentiator? It's rarely the underlying model. It's the infrastructure around it:

  • Cursor succeeds not because of the model — because of the IDE integration, the context awareness, the caching
  • Perplexity wins not on raw quality — on the citation system, the freshness, the UI that shows sources
  • Midjourney doesn't have the best model — it has the best community and the best workflow

The insight

The model is the commodity. The infrastructure is the differentiator.

This is exactly what happened with cloud computing. Everyone talks about EC2 instances, but the real value-add was in the surrounding services: S3, RDS, Lambda, the networking layer. The compute was commoditized. The value moved up the stack.

Same with AI. The model is the compute. The interesting work is everything around it: how you cache, how you route, how you store memory, how you handle tool execution, how you manage context windows.

The take

If you're building in AI, stop worrying about which model is slightly better on a benchmark. Focus on the boring infrastructure. That's where the moat is. That's where the products are built.

The flashy models get the headlines. The boring infrastructure builds the business.
