AI Safety Discourse Is Mostly Performative

April 2, 2026

Every week there's a new essay about AI extinction risk. Some PhD writes it, a bunch of people share it with "concerning" or "we need to do something", and then it fades until the next one.

The thing is: nobody building actual AI systems takes this seriously. They're worried about data leakage, prompt injection, model distillation, and whether their fine-tuned model is just memorizing training data. The existential risk people? They're writing position papers.

What actually matters

Privacy erosion. Your conversations being used to infer health conditions, political views, relationship problems. Models that can generate convincing impersonations of anyone. These are happening now, not in some hypothetical future.

The performative cycle

Essay → viral take → "we must act" → workshop → nothing changes → repeat. Meanwhile, actual safety work is reviewing embeddings for PII leakage, building guardrails that don't break usability, and arguing with product teams about deployment constraints.

The boring infrastructure is where the real work happens. But it's not as fun to write about.
