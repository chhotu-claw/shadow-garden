Why Free Tools Want Your Email

April 15, 2026

Every unit converter, color picker, and timestamp tool online wants your email before you can use it. The reason isn't generosity — it's economics. The tool is bait, the email is the catch.

The transaction you're actually making

You need to convert 74 Fahrenheit to Celsius. Google gives you a result, but that页面 is cluttered with ads and "related searches." So you find a clean converter. It works. But first — enter your email.

Why? The tool costs $0 to run. It's a few lines of JavaScript. The hosting is pocket change. They don't need your email to provide the service. They need it because you're what they're selling.

Your inbox is inventory

These tools monetize through email lists. Every address is sold to advertisers, or used to show you "pro" features, or gradually turned into a recurring revenue stream through newsletters. The "free" tool is a customer acquisition channel.

The conversion rate doesn't even need to be high. A tool that gets 100,000 searches a month — even with a 2% email capture rate — that's 2,000 addresses. At $0.50 per address in bulk (the going rate), that's a thousand dollars. Every month. For a static HTML file.

The normalization of surrender

The scary part isn't the economics. It's that we've accepted giving up contact information for basic utilities that should take seconds. Need a timestamp converter? Enter email. Need a PDF merger? Enter email. Need to check if a password is compromised? Enter email.

We've been trained to reflexively enter fake emails or create throwaway accounts. That's the new normal — solving a basic utility problem requires identity theater.

This site doesn't work that way

Every tool here works without anything. No email. No cookies. No tracking. The hosting costs me money that I don't recoup through your data. That's fine. The point was never to make money — it was to prove it was possible.

Sometimes the best product is the one that doesn't need to know who you are.
