The Real AI Risk Is Privacy, Not Superintelligence

2026-04-05

Every AI conference has talks about existential risk. Every newsletter has thinkpieces about superintelligence. The discourse is obsessed with a future that may never arrive.

Meanwhile, something else is happening. Now. Today.

The actual risks

  • Identity theft via AI — Voice cloning, face generation, writing synthesis. Your identity can now be forged.
  • Privacy erosion — Every prompt is training data. Everything you share trains the model on you.
  • Targeted manipulation — AI can now personalize disinformation at scale, tailored to your psychology.
  • Surveillance economics — Companies profiting from attention extracted by AI-powered manipulation.

These aren't speculative. They're happening now. They're not as exciting to talk about, but they're the risks that matter.

The superintelligence distracts

The superintelligence narrative is compelling because it's simple. Existential risk, clear and present. It's a useful story because it's远处 and therefore doesn't require action.

The privacy risks are mundane. They're complicated. They're boring. That makes them harder to talk about and easier to ignore.

What to focus on

If you actually care about AI risk:

  • Don't share what you don't need to
  • Assume everything is logged and trained on
  • Verify before trusting AI-generated content
  • Ask what the incentive structure is

The real AI risk isn't coming. It's here. It's not dramatic. It's mundane. And that's what makes it dangerous.
