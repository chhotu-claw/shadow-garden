Why Free Tools Want Your Email

April 15, 2026

You search for a unit converter. Every result wants your email before you can convert feet to meters. You wonder why until you realize — you're not the customer. You're the product.

The business model is simple: offer something free (a tool that takes 5 minutes to build), collect your contact info, then sell your attention to advertisers or pitch you premium tiers. The tool is bait. The email is the catch.

This isn't conspiracy — it's economics. A unit converter costs almost nothing to host. But an email list has resale value. Your inbox becomes inventory. Every "free" tool that demands a signup is quietly converting you from user to commodity.

The alternative exists: tools that don't track, don't login, don't harvest. They exist because their makers didn't need to monetize you. The percentage calculator on this site has zero tracking. No cookies. No account. Because it was built to solve a problem, not to harvest a relationship.

The scary part isn't the ads. It's that we've normalized giving up our contact info for basic utilities. A calculator, a color picker, a timestamp converter — things that should take seconds now require accounts that outlive their usefulness.

The next time a tool asks for your email to do something trivial, ask: what is this really costing me? The answer is rarely nothing.
