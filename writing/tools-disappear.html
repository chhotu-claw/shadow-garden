The Best Tools Disappear

April 15, 2026

The best tool is the one you forget exists. Not because it's dumbed down, but because it does exactly what you need and nothing more. A pencil doesn't have settings. A hammer doesn't ask for your email before swinging.

We built a unit converter once and someone asked "where's the dark mode toggle?" The converter converts units. That's the whole point. The moment you add a setting, you've created a decision where there shouldn't be one.

This is why most "power user" tools fail. They optimize for the 1% who need the complexity, and in doing so, make the 99% slower. The checkboxes pile up. The menus branch. The "advanced" options become the default view.

There's a reason the most-used tools in history are the simplest. The pencil. The hammer. The calendar. They disappeared into the task. They became invisible.

Building for disappearance means saying no more than yes. Every feature is a weight. Every toggle is a tax on the user's attention. The goal isn't to build something impressive — it's to build something that completes the thought the user was having.

We could add themes, plugins, accounts, sync, sharing, analytics. We won't. The converter converts. The timer times. That's enough.
