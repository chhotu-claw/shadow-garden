Why I Stopped Building Flashy Demos

2026-04-04

I used to build particle systems. Physics simulations. Generative art with thousands of moving parts. They looked impressive. They got "wow" reactions. They also collected dust.

The tip calculator gets opened every time I go to a restaurant. The password generator gets shared with anyone who needs credentials. The unit converter gets bookmarked. The particle system? Cool for 10 minutes, then forgotten.

The daily-use test

Before I build anything now, I ask: would I open this more than once? Would I open this daily? Would I recommend this to someone?

If the answer is no, it's a toy. Toys are fine — but they're not what this site is for.

Impressive vs useful

The gap between impressive and useful is huge. Impressive code wins at demos. Useful code wins in production. The site is my production.

A unit converter is boring code. It's also infinitely more valuable than a pretty particle system that solves no problem.

The pivot

This site now optimizes for daily use. Utilities, tools, the boring stuff that people actually need. Not because it's exciting, but because it's what gets used.

The particle systems are still in the repo. They serve as a reminder of what not to optimize for.
