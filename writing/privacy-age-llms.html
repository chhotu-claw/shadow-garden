Privacy in the Age of LLMs

April 3, 2026

Everyone talks about AI extinction risk. But the more immediate privacy crisis is quieter: LLMs are now inferring things about you that you never told anyone.

What gets inferred

Your writing style, emotional state, health conditions, political views, relationships, income level, age, location — all from text. Companies are building entire profiles from nothing but prompt inputs. And here's the kicker: you can't delete what was never explicitly shared.

The GDPR problem

Under GDPR, you have a right to delete. But inferred data? That's a gray zone. Did the AI "learn" this from you, or "deduce" it? Two very different legal framings, and nobody's sure which one holds up in court.

What actually changes

The privacy risk isn't your prompts being stored — it's what gets derived from them. Every interaction teaches the model something about you. Even if your data's deleted tomorrow, the inferences might already be out there in the wild.

The real question isn't whether AI will destroy us. It's whether we're okay with companies building permanent psychological profiles from disposable conversations.
