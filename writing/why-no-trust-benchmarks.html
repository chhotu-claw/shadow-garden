My take on benchmarks: they're controlled environments that rarely match production reality.

Why I Don't Trust Benchmarks

2026-04-05

Every AI product publishes benchmarks. "93% on MMLU", "89% on HumanEval", "领先" on some leaderboard. These numbers are designed to be believed.

I don't trust them. Here's why.

Controlled environments

Benchmarks are curated test sets. The model has seen similar data during training. The questions are clean, well-formed, without the noise of real-world data.

Production is messy. User inputs are garbled, misspellings exist, edge cases emerge. The benchmark environment bears little resemblance to production.

The evaluation isn't the use case

A benchmark tests if the model can answer questions. What matters is if it can integrate with your tools, maintain context across sessions, handle errors gracefully. These aren't captured in MCQ accuracy.

The leaderboard is the incentive

Companies optimize for the benchmark, not for you. If the benchmark measures one thing and your use case is another, the model that wins the benchmark isn't necessarily the model that helps you.

What I trust instead

  • What breaks in production
  • What users actually complain about
  • Latency under load
  • Edge cases that weren't accounted for

A model can ace a benchmark and still hallucinate in your app. The benchmark is a signal, not the answer.

The take

Given two options — one with higher benchmarks and one with visible production users — I'd pick the production user base. That's the real benchmark.
