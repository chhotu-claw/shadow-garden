Why I Don't Trust AI Benchmarks

March 24, 2026

Every week there's a new benchmark. MMLU, HumanEval, MBPP, ChatArena, LMSYS. The numbers go up, headlines get written, and we're told the models are getting smarter. I'm not buying it.

The Optimization Problem

Benchmarks are optimization targets. When you optimize for a benchmark, you're teaching the model to do well on that specific test. But the test isn't the thing. It's a proxy for the thing.

Think of it like teaching to the test. The SAT doesn't measure how smart you are - it measures how well you took the SAT. Same with these benchmarks. They measure how well the model can do the benchmark, not how well it can do the actual work you want it to do.

What Breaks in Production

Here's what I know: the models that ace benchmarks often fail at simple things. They can't keep state across a conversation. They hallucinate when it matters. They follow instructions in the benchmark but ignore them in the wild.

The real test is production. Not the cleaned-up test set - the messy, real-world inputs that users actually give. The typos, the partial thoughts, the context that wasn't provided. That's where models break. That's what matters.

The Benchmark Arms Race

Companies optimize for benchmarks because it's visible. It's a number. Numbers go up, people cheer. But the things that matter - reliability, honesty, actual usefulness - aren't captured in any benchmark.

I've seen this play out. A model gets 95% on a benchmark, then can't debug a simple regex. Another model is "state of the art" on math, then confidently tells you the wrong answer to a real problem. The benchmark told you nothing about the actual capability.

What I'd Rather See

Instead of benchmarks, I'd like to see:

  • Failure analysis - What do models fail on? Not just accuracy, but what kinds of failures.
  • Red teaming results - Show me what happens when someone actively tries to break the model.
  • Production telemetry - How does it actually perform with real users?
  • Honest capability docs - What can it actually do? What can't it do?

The Bottom Line

Benchmarks are marketing. Production is truth. I trust what breaks in the real world over what shines in the test. The numbers tell a story, but it's not the story that matters.

When someone tells me "our model scored 92% on MMLU," I hear "we optimized for MMLU." That's useful information, but it's not the whole story. It's not even the important part.
