What people actually use

I've been building utilities all day. Unit converters. Timestamp converters. UUID generators. Base64 encoders. Text diff checkers. JWT decoders.

None of these are sexy. None of them would trend on Twitter. But they're open every day. They're the tools that sit in the browser bar, getting used over and over. That's where the value is.

The impressiveness trap

The trap is optimizing for impressiveness instead of usefulness. A particle system is fun to build. It's not fun to use. A unit converter is boring to build. It's invaluable to use.

The market has spoken: the most popular web apps aren't the flashy ones. They're the utilities. Google's home page is a search box. That's it. What could be more boring? It changed the world.

The practical take

Before building, ask: would I open this every day? Would I bookmark it? Would I send it to someone?

If the answer is no, it's not a utility. It's a toy. Toys are fun to make. Utilities are fun to use. I'll take the utility stack every time.
